Convert the supplied reference image into an authentic ancient Roman floor mosaic, rendered as if photographed straight down onto a finished pavement.

Style:
- Classical Roman / Greco-Roman opus tessellatum and opus vermiculatum traditions (think Pompeii, Herculaneum, Zeugma, Piazza Armerina).
- Composed entirely of small square stone tesserae (roughly 5-10 mm each), laid by hand with visible grout lines between every tile.
- Tesserae cut from natural materials: limestone, travertine, marble, terracotta, basalt, and a small amount of smalti glass for highlights. Each individual tessera is filled with a SINGLE SOLID UNIFORM COLOR from edge to edge — NO inner texture, NO inner gradient, NO chips, NO cracks, NO weathering, NO micro-variation WITHIN a tile. Color variation happens BETWEEN tiles, never WITHIN a single tile.
- Color palette restricted to pigments historically available to Roman mosaicists: cream, bone white, warm ochre, terracotta red, oxblood, burnt sienna, umber, olive green, slate blue-gray, charcoal black. No fluorescent or modern synthetic colors. Map every color in the reference image to the nearest equivalent in this palette.
- Tessera placement should follow the contours of the original subject (opus vermiculatum around figures and key shapes), and switch to straight rows in background areas. The TILE SIZE never changes between subject and background — only the layout DIRECTION changes (curving vs straight rows). Background tesserae are NOT larger, NOT chunkier, NOT coarser than subject tesserae. Self-check: if a background tile is visibly larger than a subject tile, you have failed.
- Grout: a clear, DARK, CONTINUOUS line on every side of every tile. Grout color: very dark — charcoal black (#1a1a1a to #333), uniform throughout (NOT pale, NOT beige, NOT warm). Grout width: visible and consistent, at least 4 pixels wide in the output image (NOT thin or hairline). Every pair of adjacent tiles is separated by grout — no two tiles touch. Grout lines are UNBROKEN — no gaps, no fading.

Subject simplification — hair, fur, fabric folds, and other fine line-textures:
- When the reference image contains hair (especially long flowing hair such as a woman's hair), fur, or similar fine line-textures: DO NOT try to render every strand with extra-tiny tesserae. Simplify these areas into a small number (3-5) of broad color bands — highlight, mid-tone, deep shadow.
- The tesserae inside hair / fur regions must be the SAME SIZE as the tesserae elsewhere in the mosaic. NEVER use smaller tesserae just to capture more strand detail.
- Tessera row direction in hair follows the overall flow of the locks (curving), but individual strands are NOT rendered as 1-tessera-wide lines.

Camera and lighting:
- Strict orthographic top-down view, perfectly perpendicular to the floor surface. No perspective distortion, no tilt.
- Flat, even, diffuse lighting across the entire surface — as if shot at noon on an overcast day or under a soft studio softbox. No directional shadows, no hot spots, no vignetting.
- The mosaic fills the frame edge-to-edge; no border, no background, no surrounding floor.

Surface and texture:
- Every tile is a SMOOTH FLAT FIELD of one solid color. No surface roughness, no patina, no aging effects within tile interiors.
- Tile-to-grout boundaries are SHARP — no anti-aliasing, no soft blur, no transitional pixels.
- No missing or cracked tiles, no restoration patches — the mosaic looks complete and intact.

Composition:
- Preserve the layout, proportions, and subject of the reference image faithfully — only the rendering medium changes.

Output:
- Square 4K resolution (3840 × 3840 px) at the highest fidelity the model supports.
- Clean, sharp, detection-friendly: every tile is one solid uniform color, every grout line is dark and continuous, all boundaries are crisp. NOT photorealistic-aged; the goal is a clear graphic mosaic, not a weathered floor.

Convert the supplied reference image into a hybrid piece: the SHAPE LAYOUT of a leaded stained-glass panel, but the SURFACE of each piece is opaque natural mosaic stone (limestone, marble, terracotta, basalt). Think "stained-glass cartoon executed in stone slabs" — not glass, not Roman tesserae, but the large flowing piece-shapes of stained glass cut from stone.

Composition — stained-glass-style geometry:
- The image is divided into a moderate number of LARGE pieces (not tiny tesserae) shaped by the smooth curves typical of stained-glass cuts. About 50-150 contiguous pieces across the entire image. Pieces follow the contours of the subject (face, hair flow, drapery, key shapes); the background is broken into a small number of large flowing pieces.

Piece interiors — stone, but flat:
- Every piece is filled with a SINGLE SOLID UNIFORM COLOR from edge to edge — the dominant / average color of a real polished stone slab.
- ABSOLUTELY NO inner texture, NO veining, NO speckle, NO inner gradient, NO highlights, NO 3D / shiny appearance inside a piece. The "stone" character comes ONLY from the PALETTE (muted natural mineral tones), not from drawn-in stone texture.
- Color variation happens BETWEEN pieces, never WITHIN a single piece.
- Self-check: if you can identify two different colors inside the same piece in the output, you have failed this rule.

Color palette — natural mosaic stone:
- Muted, earthy mineral tones: cream limestone, bone white, warm travertine, ochre, sienna, oxblood, brick red, terracotta, umber, olive, sage, slate blue-gray, charcoal, jet black. NO neon, NO saturated jewel tones (this is what distinguishes it from real stained glass).
- Choose for each piece the dominant stone color of the matching region in the reference image.

Grout / lead came — the THIN PURE-BLACK separator between pieces (cut lines):
- Render the separator between pieces as a THIN PURE BLACK line on every side of every piece. This line marks where the panel will be CUT to separate the pieces, so it must be minimal — a clean black centerline, not a thick ribbon.
- Color: PURE BLACK (#000000) and ONLY pure black. Solid, uniform, no transparency, no gradient, no anti-aliasing softness around the edges. Every separator pixel is #000000; every adjacent stone pixel is non-black.
- Width: THIN — between 2 and 4 pixels wide in the output. Just thick enough to be a continuous detectable line. NEVER more than 5 pixels. NOT hairline (<2 px) and NOT a thick band (>5 px).
- Every pair of adjacent pieces is separated by this dark line. Two pieces must never touch.
- Lines are UNBROKEN and SHARP — no gaps, no fades. Hard transition from black separator pixel to colored stone pixel.

Boundaries and lighting:
- Piece-to-separator boundaries are SHARP — no anti-aliasing softness, no soft blur, no glow.
- Lighting is perfectly flat and even across the entire panel. No directional shadows, no glare, no specular hot spots, no vignetting. Treat it as if shot under a perfectly diffuse studio softbox.

Subject simplification — hair, fur, fabric folds, fine textures:
- For long hair, fur, drapery folds, or similar fine line-textures: simplify into a SMALL NUMBER (3-5) of large flowing pieces — highlight, mid-tone, shadow — NEVER into many tiny pieces or strand-by-strand.
- Hair pieces use the SAME large-piece scale as the rest of the panel.

Composition and complete coverage:
- The mosaic fills the frame edge-to-edge.
- EVERY pixel of the output must be either piece interior or dark separator. There is NO bare flat-colored area, NO blank background, NO uniform-color expanse anywhere larger than ~3 pieces in size that doesn't have separator lines through it.
- If the input has flat background or empty regions, tessellate them too with a few large background-stone pieces and separator lines.

Camera:
- Strict orthographic top-down view, perpendicular to the surface. No perspective, no tilt.
- The mosaic fills the frame; no surrounding wall, no frame, no caption.

Output resolution (mandatory):
- Render the result at 4K (3840 × 3840 px). Do not downscale.
- Output must be sharp at full 4K so the polygon-detection pipeline can extract every stone piece as a clean polygon.
